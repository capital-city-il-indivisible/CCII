description: "Coined by civil rights icon Congressman John Lewis, Good Trouble is the act of coming together to take peaceful, non violent action to challenge injustice. The power of collective non violent action resulted in the passage of the Voting Rights Act of 1965 - and we need that same collective action to fight for voting rights today. Because they know our country is strongest when voters show up , speak out and exercise our power, politicians in Congress and in state legislatures have pushed multiple voter suppression bills. Aided by the Supreme Court’s disastrous decision to gut the Voting Rights Act, states are now rushing in maps designed to stifle black and brown voting power. The way we honor Congressman Lewis’ life and work is to organize, educate and take action. Please join us!
This year’s Good Trouble Lives On (GTLO) forum is part of the 2026 Survival Revival - a collective effort by community leaders, activists, organizers and residents to meet the immediate needs of our neighbors while building a stronger, more resilient Springfield. In this moment of both crisis and opportunity our community can do more than simply survive the challenges we face. We are working together to strengthen our connections, build community power and create a city where everyone has the opportunity not just to survive, but to thrive. Throughout the afternoon there will be direct action opportunities, education, and engagement around the following issues:
• Police reform and accountability
• Voter education, rights and civic engagement (GTLO Forum)
• Mutual aid and community support networks"
